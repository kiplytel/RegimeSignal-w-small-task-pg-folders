How the MRS Score Works
The Market Regime Score combines 8 proprietary core factors (69.01% weight) with 17 sub-core signal amplifiers (30.99%) into a single 0–100 composite score. Scores map to 7 market stages with key thresholds at 44 (Bear Alert) and 55 (Bull Market). Today's reading of 47.3 places us in the Neutral—Cautionary Zone, only 3.3 points above Bear Alert.
CORE 8 FACTORS — 69.01% WEIGHT
The Core 8 are proprietary macro and market-structure factors that historically precede regime shifts: Federal Reserve Policy, Government Policy, Corporate Earnings, Liquidity & Financial Conditions, Inflation, Consumer Sentiment, Credit Spreads, and Market Breadth. Each is scored 0–100 using rolling historical percentiles, then combined via proprietary weights calibrated on 40+ years of S&P 500 regime data.
SUB-CORE 17 — 30.99% WEIGHT
Seventeen signal amplifiers that detect acceleration, divergence, and confirmation around the Core 8. These include volatility regime, yield curve slope, high-yield spreads, put/call flows, advance–decline dynamics, and momentum persistence. The Sub-Core acts as an early-warning layer — it can pull the composite score down ahead of the Core 8 when conditions deteriorate quickly.
THE 7 MARKET STAGES
Scores map to: Strong Bull (78–100), Moderate Bull (65–77), Marginal Bull (55–64), Neutral / Bull Breach (45–54), Bear Alert (35–44), Moderate Bear (22–34), Critical Bear (0–21). Thresholds were derived from 40+ years of back-testing to optimize bear detection (≤44 triggers defensive posture) and bull recovery signals (≥55 confirms re-entry).
INTERPRETATION
MRS is a regime classification tool, not a price prediction or timing signal. A score of 47.3 means macro and market conditions are mixed — neither confirmed bull nor bear. The system is designed to keep capital defensive when the score is ≤44 and aggressive-balanced when ≥55. The 45–54 zone is explicitly a "watch" zone requiring weekly monitoring.
